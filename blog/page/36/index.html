Nimbupani Version 3.0

Feb 20th, 2005 | Comments

After two weeks of dilly dallying, I finally managed to finish up the redesign that I have been itching to do for the past two months. The colophon gives you an idea of the technical aspects of redesign.

As you can see all the categories have vanished for good. They didnt make sense anyway. I am proud of the use of php and css for the active links that you see on the left, possibly I might even write down a tutorial!

I also used Rounded Corners for the rounded rectangle you see. It isnt perfect. No solution suited my requirements well as my design does not have a plain background like the rest of the solutions.

Colophon

Feb 19th, 2005 | Comments

I started nimbupani in 2003 when two of my closest friends started to blog. It was my first attempt at web design and writing. Previous incarnations were consequently slightly uncomforting to look at, but I am glad to say I have looked and learned a bit since then.

The Name

Nimbupani means literally “Lemon water” in hindi.Nimbupani, is a favoured drink in India especially during the deliriously hot season (which seems to be replacing the rest of the seasons in India). Nimbupani is supposed to be pronounced nimbupaani. Nimbupani is cool, refreshing and revitalizes you for the long day ahead. I personally prefer buttermilk though. But buttermilk isnt a cool name to give to a website, is it?

Design

I am guilty of all the artwork, design that you see on this site. I have hopefully compiled with the latest standards and if the site doesnt validate, just holler.

This website uses PHP and CSS for ease of use. The links that you see on the right are a separate PHP page which in combination with CSS is used to hilight the current active page.

Typography

If you are a Mac user, congrats! You can experience the power of Lucida Grande while Windows users can stick to the pathetic Franklin Gothic Medium. I cant help it if Windows sucks.

Machines

Designed on a Mac Mini using trial versions of Dreamweaver and Fireworks MX.

Content Management System

I use MovableType 3.15 to power this website. The plugins I use on this site are:

Your Privacy

Relax, I aint a spammer and I havent sold my soul to the devil (yet). Your information is safe with me (heck! I dont think I will even remember if you left your identification or not!). Relax and write your comments without any fear of Big Brothers.

Supported Browsers

This site looks and works the best in Safari or Firefox. You can also view it in IE 6. It has not been tested on Opera, Mozilla, Netscape, Konqueror and the unashamedly evil IE 5.

Black

Feb 9th, 2005 | Comments

I think for the first time in the history of India cinema we have actors who have acted, a wee bit theaterical but nonetheless it was excellence that was on display.

The film starts with an amazingly touching voice over by Rani Mukherjee with a black screen where she asks “how long can you live like this? minutes, hours? I live like this all my life.” (pardon my poor translation).

I wouldnt like to bore you with the story line but I really liked the fact that this film covers some unknown territory unlike any other movie about the deaf and blind. Nobody has ever asked whether the blind have any sexual aspirations, we assume they have no right to, which is very heartless. But this movie has very poignantly raised that question and left the answer for us to interpret.

I was astounded to know Amitabh Bachchan can act. I didnt know he had it in him to execute this role to perfection. He has beaten all hollywood stars hollow in this movie. I think he should win the Oscar for Best Actor (I know I know it is impossible, but I think he deserves one).

So get your asses off and grab the once in a lifetime opportunity of watching a real good hindi movie.
