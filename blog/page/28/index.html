The Raging Ruins

Aug 3rd, 2005

I went for dinner with Badri yesterday and for want of change and healthy lifestyle decided to try the Yogi Hub at Boon Tat Street. Boon Tat Street is most famous for Lau Pa Sat, but I was shocked to see an old islamic style building boarded u p with “Danger” boards.

Shocked to see Singapore Government has allowed a ruin to continue in the middle of the city without the typical Singaporean “brand new” look. Pleased to know that Singapore does have buildings that are ancient and not made to look new. Buildings that have stood the test of time and have made it successfully to the future. Buildings that indicate that Singapore was not a miracle city created by androids ( a la matrix) but a city that grew up through struggle and determination.

Singapore does have a human face behind the plastic smile.

P.S. Does anybody know what that building is?

Update Just found out from Street Directory that it is the Nagore Durga Shrine, a mosque constructed by the migrants from Southern India.

Mumbai Monsoons

Jul 29th, 2005

When I heard about people taken at floods in Mumbai, I dismissed them as Typical Indian Monsoon News. Then, when I stumbled upon Amit Verma’s blog, I got to see the full force of nature’s fury.

But worse is the callousness of humanity. People dying of getting electrocuted, landslides and building collapses. Mumbai, already a very dirty city, is just about to get dirtier and more disease prone. Stagnant water, thanks to pathetic drainage systems, is gonna cause more disasters than the catastrophic rains.

Those who are dead will rest in peace, but it is us, who let them die of frivolous reasons, who wont. More resources are on Cloudburst Mumbai.
